Formulation technologies

Gattefossé can help you determine the lipid excipients you are seeking for and give you useful tips to process them.

Oral

Application

  • Excipients for solubility and bioavailability enhancement
  • Excipients for lubrication
  • Excipients for modified release
  • Excipients for taste masking and API protection

Process and formulation

  • Lipid-based formulations
  • Direct compression
  • Granulation
  • Capsule filling and molding
  • Hot melt extrusion
  • Hot melt coating
  • High shear coating
  • 3D printing

Topical and transdermal

Application

  • Solubilizing drugs for skin delivery, a decisive step
  • Selecting penetration enhancers, for transdermal delivery
  • Opting for the best emulsifier, as a function of the formulation
  • Adjusting consistency of topical dosage forms
  • Providing emolliency with oily vehicles
  • Developing patient-friendly textures

Process and formulation

  • Caring for patients
  • Bi-gel, opening up innovation potential
  • Cream, the most popular topical dosage form
  • Emulgel, combining emulsion and gel benefits
  • Foam, an emerging topical dosage form
  • Hydrogel, in full transparency
  • Lotion, low viscosity for high sprayability
  • Microemulsion, a stunning potential
  • Ointment, for damaged skins
  • Patch, for controlled skin drug delivery
  • Stick, spotting the difference
